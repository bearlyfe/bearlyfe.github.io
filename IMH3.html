DEALING WITH MENTAL ILLNESSES

Mental illnesses are quite common in society and they can affect people of different backgrounds. For people who are unfortunate enough to experience them, they can be quite impairing. A person may not be able to study or work anymore due to an onset of illness. They may have issues dealing with other people and have troublesome relationships. They may also develop a risk of violence, self-neglect, and self-harm. Such is the extent of how serious mental illnesses can become.

Just as other physical illnesses deserve medical attention, mental illnesses also deserve medical attention. When there are initial symptoms, people should seek medical attention by seeing their family doctor or psychiatrist, such that there is no delay in treatment.

When seeking treatment, there is pharmacological therapy as well as non-pharmacological therapy. Doctors prescribe treatment according to the severity of the illness. Pharmacological therapy aims to change a chemical imbalance such that the symptoms may be controlled. Non-pharmacological therapy aims to heal a person by a relationship and rapport between the therapist and the patient. With treatment, mental illness can be controlled and the patient may regain a normal functioning life.

It is important to remember not to keep a mental illness hidden. Let people around you who care about you know about it. They may be able to provide support and help you through hard times.
