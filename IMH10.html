SELF-HARM ≠ SUICIDE

Each of us go through hard times, but different people cope differently. Some people cope by harming themselves. Bear in mind self-harm is not a suicide attempt, but actually a way to cope with really painful feelings. People may use self-harm to:

· Relieve tension

· Release adrenaline, which is a chemical that temporarily lowers pain

· Replace emotional pain with physical pain

· Give themselves a sense of control over their life, even though they might feel like the behaviour is controlling them.

 

Cutting is one form of self-harm. Cutting can have risks including excess bleeding or getting an infection. Whilst self-harm is often the result of feeling out of control and not a suicide attempt, there is a chance someone could accidentally cut too deep which could result in death. Other ways of self-harm include: snapping an elastic band against the wrist, drawing on the body with red marker, or squeezing ice cubes in the hands. These can have similar effects without breaking the skin.

Because there are risks with self-harm, it’s important to explore healthier ways of coping. If someone you know is using self-harm as a way to cope with stress, it would be great for you to remind them that there are other ways to deal with hard times. It is not easy coping with stress, but there are definitely different ways. Maybe finding someone to talk to or pursuing a hobby could be what the person needs. Explore with them the things that you can do to help them and how they are best able to help themselves.
