EFFECTS OF CANNABIS

Now that we have the terms locked down, let’s talk about the “science” behind Cannabis! The active ingredient of this drug is Tetrahydrocannabinol (THC). THC is able to interact with endogenous cannabinoid receptors, which are specific brain cell receptors that react to natural THC-like chemicals. This induces Dopamine release and over-activates the part of the brain that creates the “high” that people feel.


Short-term effects of Cannabis:

• Relaxation

• Euphoria

• Relaxed inhibitions

• Sense of well-being

• Disorientation

• Altered time and space perception

• Lack of concentration, impaired learning and memory

• Drowsiness

• Sedation

• Mood changes such as panic reactions and paranoia

• A more vivid sense of taste, sight, smell, and hearing

• Flights of fragmentary thoughts

• A dulling of attention despite an illusion of heightened insight

• Image distortion

• Psychosis (psychosis during intoxication, acute psychosis beyond intoxication, persistent psychosis)

Acute physical effects:

• Increased heart rate

• Reddening of the eyes

• Dry mouth and throat

• Increased appetite

• Vasodilatation


Medical use of Cannabis?

Scientific study of the THC in Cannabis has led to two FDA-approved medications that contain cannabinoid chemicals in pill form (Marinol and Dronabinol). Marinol is indicated for the treatment of anorexia (loss of appetite) in patients with AIDS, and to treat nausea and vomiting associated with cancer therapy. Recently, there is another FDA-approved medication – Epidiolex (cannabidiol CBD) oral solution that is used to treat seizures associated with two rare and severe forms of epilepsy, Lennox-Gastaut syndrome and Dravet syndrome, in patients two years of age and older. With ongoing research into Cannabis, it may not be a surprise that we develop more medical treatment usages of Cannabis in future years. However all these drugs are not licensed drugs in Hong Kong.

Bearlyfe Advice:

Apart from getting imprisoned for seven years if found having possession or having smoked/inhaled/ingest cannabis, any person who cultivates any plant of the genus cannabis or opium poppy, is liable upon conviction to a fine of HK$ 100,000 and imprisonment for fifteen years!
