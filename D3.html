THE ICY TRUTH OF ICE (PART ONE)

To make sure we’re on the same page – the abusive Ice here is not referring to ice we have in our drinks, but crystal meth, which is another popular class of illicit drugs. Methamphetamine is a stimulant drug that can be ingested as white powder or pill, while crystal methamphetamine is the form of the drug ingested as glass fragments. It is related to Amphetamine and acts as stimulant that induces dopamine release. Meth is known for its addictive stimulant properties, which can cause a huge rush of the ‘pleasure chemical’ dopamine into the brain. Meth’s impact should not be underestimated, it can in fact release three times more dopamine than cocaine and the effects can last from eight to twelve hours, compared to around two hours for cocaine. This class of drugs is also known to be used for the treatment of narcolepsy, attention-deficit hyperactivity disorders (ADHD), and obesity.

People usually take in meth by inhaling/smoking, snorting, injection, swallowing, or inserted rectally. Interestingly, Meth is common among young users due to its relatively cheap price, making it more readily accessible for young people.

Effects of Meth on the body

Acute mental effects of Meth

• Pleasurable euphoria

• A heightened level of alertness

• Increased energy

• Increased libido and enhanced sexual pleasure

• Psychotic symptoms in high dose

• Prolonged sleep and mild dysphoria occur as the drug effects wear off

Apart from the known mental effect of Meth, it induces serious physical harm as well. Some acute physical effects of Meth include:  

• Anorexia

• Hypertension

• Hyperthermia

• Heart attack

• Renal failure

• Seizures

• Death, usual lethal dose is 0.1 – 1.7 gram

Since Meth is highly addictive, when people stop using Meth it is associated with strong withdrawal symptoms:

• Depressed mood, anxiety

• Disturbed sleep

• Reduced energy

• Cognitive impairment

• Craving

• Obsessive grinding of teeth “meth teeth”, that ultimately require extraction of all teeth!
