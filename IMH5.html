MY FRIEND HAS MENTAL ILLNESS

There have been many misconceptions concerning mental illnesses in human history. People have been ignorant of its disease status until fairly recently. Now, it is known to us that mental illnesses are illnesses just like diseases of our body. They require treatment and management by mental health professionals. Therefore, it is essential l that we encourage those with mental illnesses to seek help. 

To achieve this, a very important aspect is that society in gross terms needs to acknowledge the vulnerability of people with mental illnesses and the fact that mental illnesses as a “real” disease. Each and every one of us has a role to play to help make a change to help people with mental illnesses seek help.

What we can all do is try to change our attitude towards mental illnesses. In order to help our friends with mental illnesses we should make some changes to the way we view mental illnesses. Below are three things we can do:

Number one: Challenge our own prejudices. Let us reflect on the perception we have about mentally ill people. What are they exactly? Do some research and determine whether or not these perceptions are justified.

Number two: Avoid stigmatizing language. When we refer to people who have mental illnesses what phrases do we use? Are there negative connotations? If there are, it would be appropriate to search for a more empathic way of expressing the same thing.

Number three: Be advocates for societal and political changes. Once we rid ourselves of prejudice, let us try to observe and identify stigma in people around us. Understand why they suffer those stigmas and try to offer them alternative explanations.

As we practise these three steps we are steering in the right path to making the world a better place for those who have mental illnesses.
